Use Case 03 · Healthcare

Cardiac Disease Risk Prediction

Machine learning applied to early cardiovascular risk detection

Case Overview

Title

Early Prediction of Cardiovascular Disease Risk

Problem Description

Heart diseases are one of the leading causes of death worldwide. Detecting cardiovascular risk early is critical to prevent serious complications such as heart attacks, heart failure, or strokes. Manual analysis of patient data is time-consuming and prone to human error at scale.

Type of Machine Learning

Supervised Learning — Binary Classification (Logistic Regression)

Expected Output

Binary classification — High Risk / Low Risk of cardiac disease

Input Data

Patient age · Systolic blood pressure · Cholesterol level · Maximum heart rate · Blood glucose level · Body Mass Index (BMI)

Real-World Relevance

Hospitals and research centers worldwide use ML models to support clinical diagnoses, reduce diagnostic errors, and enable personalized medicine. Early risk prediction allows preventive treatment before symptoms appear, saving lives and reducing healthcare costs.

01 — Context

Heart diseases are one of the leading causes of death worldwide. Early detection of cardiovascular risk helps prevent serious complications such as heart attacks, heart failure, or strokes.

02 — Machine Learning in medicine

Machine Learning is a branch of artificial intelligence that allows systems to automatically learn from historical data without being explicitly programmed with rules.

In the medical field, these models analyze large amounts of clinical information to identify patterns that may indicate the presence or risk of a disease.

03 — How the prediction model works

The model receives different medical variables from the patient as input data. Based on thousands of previous cases, the algorithm learns which combinations of factors increase the probability of heart disease.

Once trained, the system analyzes new data and classifies the patient into one of two outcomes:

High Risk Low Risk

04 — Variables analyzed

  • Patient age
  • Systolic blood pressure
  • Cholesterol level
  • Maximum heart rate
  • Blood glucose level
  • Body Mass Index (BMI)

05 — Type of model used

Supervised classification

The algorithm learns from labeled data where it is known whether the patient had heart disease or not.

This project applies Logistic Regression — a mathematical model that calculates the probability of a specific event occurring, in this case the development of heart disease.

06 — Importance of early prediction

Early identification of risk allows the application of preventive treatments, lifestyle changes, and timely medical follow-up.

The use of Machine Learning in medicine also contributes to personalized healthcare, where each patient receives recommendations based on their own clinical data.

07 — Real-world applications

Hospitals and research centers currently use artificial intelligence models to support diagnoses, analyze medical images, and predict risks of chronic diseases.

These systems do not replace doctors — they serve as decision-support tools to improve clinical decision-making.
